Pneumatic elevators

These elevators take up less space in the building and move using air pressure. Essentially a small tube within a large tube, these elevators aren’t unlike that of a bank deposit tube. However, the small size of these elevators come at a cost. Although elevators are 20 times safer than escalators, pneumatic elevators are only capable of carrying 550 lbs at a given time.

Traction elevators

These elevators are the least expensive of residential elevator types, but they also move the slowest. They work by moving up and down a track using a counterweight. Despite their speed, these elevators are often favored because they don’t require a machine room.

Hydraulic elevators

These elevators take up more space than the other types, but they also move considerably faster and make less noise. Hydraulic elevators work through the use of a hydraulic tank and controller.

Geared Traction Elevator

The geared elevator is made up of a motor that has a gearbox attached to it. The main function of the gears is to power the wheel that moves the ropes. This type of elevator can travel up to speeds of up to 500 feet per minute. The maximum distance it can travel is 250 feet.
